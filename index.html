Chang'e 5 Mission A Complete Success

17.12.2020

The Chang'e 5 (pronounced 'chang erh five') lunar sample return mission has concluded with a successful return to Earth. Launched on November 23, the mission involved a soft landing on the moon followed by the gathering of samples using both drilling and surface scooping. These samples were then launch into lunar orbit and transferred to the return capsule which successfully landed a few hundred kilometres from Beijing last night.

Details of the New RWS Revealed

15.12.2020

During an exercise conducted by the 53rd Brigade, the universal remote weapons system that equips the CSK-series of light armoured vehicles was presented in unprecedented detail. Key capabilities demonstrated include its quick-attach/detach capability and the two views available at the control console; an unzoomed general observation view and a zoomed scope view.

New Man-Portable Thermobaric Rocket

14.12.2020

A chemical-defence regiment in the XJ Military District conducted live exercises with a new thermobaric man-portable rocket system. The system appears to have two warhead options; incendiary and demolition. The former leaves a trail of fiery residue at the impact site while the latter produces a large explosion that generates a highly destructive shockwave demolishing targets in the vicinity.

Z-20 Spotted at More Brigades

13.12.2020

The Z-20 10t-class utility helicopter, hitherto only seen flying with the two aerial assault brigades in 75th and 83rd GA, has been spotted within the 85th and 76th Army Aviation brigades, signifying the aircraft's introduction to regular aviation brigades. The Z-20 is slated to become the PLA's main utility helicopter and will contribute to alleviating the chronic helicopter shortage.
